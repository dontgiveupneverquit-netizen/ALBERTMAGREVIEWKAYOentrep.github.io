Lesson 2

Marketing Mix

The set of tools or elements a business uses to promote and sell its product or service effectively.

Key words

Tools Elements Strategies Promote Sell

Purpose / importance

01
Decide what product to sell
02
Decide how much to charge
03
Decide where to sell
04
Decide how to promote

Simple meaning

Marketing Mix = combination of strategies to meet customer needs and achieve business goals.

Origin of the marketing mix

1960
E. Jerome McCarthy introduced the 4Ps.
Product, Price, Place, Promotion.
1981
Booms and Bitner expanded it to 7Ps.
Added People, Process, Physical Evidence to address services and interaction.

The 7Ps of Marketing Mix

1. Product

Anything a business offers to satisfy customer needs or wants.

Goods (tangible): smartphones, shoes, bottled water, clothing.
Services (intangible): haircut, tutoring, food delivery, car repair.

2. Price

Price is how much customers pay. It affects how they see value and quality.

Competitive — match, lower, or slightly higher than competitors.
Penetration — low starting price to attract customers fast.
Skimming — high starting price, then lower over time.
Value-added — higher price for extra features or benefits.
Psychological — price endings that feel cheaper (e.g., ₱999).
Dynamic — prices change based on demand, supply, and behavior.
Promotional — temporary discounts or limited-time deals.
Cost-plus — add markup to cost.
Formula: Selling Price = Unit Cost + (Unit Cost x Markup Percentage).

3. Place

How and where the product is made available.

Direct: Manufacturer to Consumer.
Indirect: Manufacturer to Wholesalers to Retailers to Consumers.

4. Promotion

How the business communicates value to customers.

Goals: awareness, interest, purchase, loyalty.
Examples: advertising, sales promos, direct marketing, personal selling.

5. People

All individuals who interact with customers or shape experience.

Employees, customer service, sales staff, business owner.

6. Process

Steps and procedures to deliver the product or service.

Order, payment, delivery, customer service processes.
Smooth process = satisfied customers.

7. Physical Evidence

Tangible proof that builds trust and shows quality.

Packaging, labels, store appearance, receipts.

Creating a brand name

Simplicity

Easy to spell and pronounce.

Relevance

Reflects the product or business values.

Uniqueness

Stands out from competitors.

Appeal

Fits the target market preferences and culture.

Future flexibility

Allows growth without rebranding.

Legal availability

Avoid trademark or copyright issues.

Lesson 3

4Ms of Operations

The essential resources needed to produce goods or deliver services.

Key words

Resources Operations Production Management

The 4Ms

1. Manpower

People involved in the business: employees, supervisors, and managers.

Includes: skills, qualifications, productivity.
Examples: cashiers, factory workers, teachers, cooks, delivery riders.

2. Method

Organized procedures and steps used to deliver products or services.

Ensures work is systematic and consistent.
Examples: cooking steps, service process, assembly flow.

3. Machine

Tools and equipment used in production or service delivery.

Examples: computers, ovens, printers, sewing machines, cash registers.

4. Materials

Raw inputs and components needed to create the output.

Affect quality, cost, and usability.
Examples: ingredients, paper, fabric, office supplies.

Prototype

Meaning

A preliminary model used for testing before mass production.

Types

Physical prototype: tangible model.
Digital prototype: virtual or simulated model.

Testing the prototype

Internal testing

Testing within the business.

User testing

Testing by potential customers.

Pilot testing

Small-scale real-world testing.

Validation

Meaning

Checks if a product is desirable, useful, and likely to be purchased.

Purpose

Meets customer needs.
Identifies strengths and weaknesses.
Reduces financial risk.
Guides improvement.

Methods of validation

Surveys

Use questionnaires to test demand.

Focus groups

Discuss with target customers.

Product demos

Sampling or live demos.

Suppliers

Meaning

Providers of materials, components, or services for production.

Selection factors

Quality.
Cost and payment terms.
Delivery reliability.
Reputation.
Capacity to meet demand.

Supply chain vs value chain

Supply chain

Steps from raw materials to finished product delivery.

Value chain

Activities that add value and improve product quality.

Parts of the supply chain

Inbound logistics, operations/production, outbound logistics.

Manpower needs

Meaning

Ensures enough workers with the right skills.

Purpose

Avoid overstaffing (high cost).
Avoid understaffing (low quality).
Identify tasks and roles.
Determine number of employees.
Identify skills and qualifications.

Recruitment

Process of finding, attracting, and hiring qualified personnel.

Advertise

Post job openings.

Screen

Review applications.

Interview

Conduct interviews/tests.

Hire & train

Onboard and train employees.

Focus: memorize the 4Ms, prototype/testing types, and supplier selection factors.

Lesson 4

Forecast the Revenue of the Business

Revenue forecasting is the process of estimating how much money a business expects to earn within a specific period. It is a prediction, not an exact amount.

Key words

Estimate Prediction Income

Factors affecting revenue

Price

Product or service price.

Sales volume

Number of units sold.

Customer demand

Preferences and buying behavior.

Marketing

Promotion and visibility.

Competition

Rivals in the market.

Location

Where the business operates.

Seasonality

Economic and seasonal changes.

Quality

Product or service quality.

Basic revenue formula

Revenue = Price x Quantity Sold

Simple assumptions used

01
Selling price
02
Expected units sold
03
Time period (day, week, month)

Sample computation (standard format)

Assumptions

Price per unit: ₱120
Units sold per day: 35
Days per week: 6
Weeks per month: 4

Daily revenue

₱120 x 35 = ₱4,200

Weekly revenue

₱4,200 x 6 = ₱25,200

Monthly revenue

₱25,200 x 4 = ₱100,800

Focus: memorize the revenue formula and be ready to compute daily, weekly, and monthly figures.
